Basic Hunting Information

What to know before your hunt — regulations, trophy care, and what camp life looks like once you arrive.

Regulations & Trophy Care

Everything squared away, so you can hunt.

1

Hunter Safety

A hunter safety card is required for all hunters born on or after January 1, 1966 in Wyoming. In South Dakota, all hunters under 16 need one.

2

Hunter Orange

During rifle seasons, a garment of hunter orange must be worn above the waist — a cap, vest, shirt or coat will do. Archery hunters are exempt.

3

Trophy Care

Caping and antler/horn removal is provided. We have an excellent local taxidermist you can leave your trophy with, or you can take it home.

4

Meat Processing

Done locally — cut, wrapped and sharp-frozen before you leave. Meat can be donated, but processing fees still apply.

5

Weather & Clothing

Weather can fluctuate considerably every season. Bring layers so you can adjust to conditions accordingly.

6

Licenses Handled

Once you book, we take care of the paperwork to obtain the points or licenses your hunt needs. See license info →

Lodging

Lodging & Food

A home-style camp with all the comforts.

Camp is clean and comfortable with all the conveniences of home. Two hunters share each bedroom, and there's complete privacy for couples. We keep a home-style atmosphere and give individual attention to everyone.

Good, healthy home-cooked meals fill the void that's sure to come from each day's hunt. Breakfast is served well before daylight so we can be in the field before shooting light. Sack lunches are enjoyed in the field to maximize hunting time, and a hearty dinner is served every evening while we sit around the table and share the day's experiences.

Alcohol isn't provided at camp but is allowed after hunting is done for the day. Intoxication will not be tolerated.
